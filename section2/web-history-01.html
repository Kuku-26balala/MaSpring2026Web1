History of the Web

1945, Memex, Vannevar Bush

The Memex was a concept described by Vannevar Bush in 1945. It was a machine that could store and organize information. Bush imagined that people could link documents together. This idea later influenced the development of hypertext and the Web.

SOURCE: Wikipedia

1960s, Project Xanadu “hypertext”, ted nelson

Project Xanadu was created by Ted Nelson in the 1960s. He introduced the idea of “hypertext,” which allows documents to link to each other. Xanadu imagined a global network of connected information. This idea strongly influenced the World Wide Web.

SOURCE: Wikipedia

1960s The birth of the Internet ARPANET

ARPANET was the first network that connected computers over long distances. It was funded by the U.S. government. Researchers used it to share information between universities. ARPANET later became the foundation of today's Internet.

SOURCE: Wikipedia

first email Ray Tomlinson 1971

Ray Tomlinson sent the first email in 1971. He was the first person to use the “@” symbol in an email address. Email made communication between computers much easier. It quickly became one of the most popular uses of the Internet.

SOURCE: Wikipedia

Transfer Control Protocol/Internetwork Protocol (TCP/IP) Vint Cerf and Robert Kahn 1983

TCP/IP is the main communication system used by the Internet. It allows different networks to connect and share data. In 1983, ARPANET adopted TCP/IP. This moment is considered the official start of the modern Internet.

SOURCE: Wikipedia

Invention of the World Wide Web Tim Berners-Lee and Robert Cailliau at the European Council for Nuclear Research (CERN) 1989

In 1989, Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web at CERN. He wanted to make it easier for scientists to share information. The Web uses links to connect documents. This invention changed the way people use the Internet.

SOURCE: Wikipedia

Foundations of a website URL /HTTP/HTML/BROWSER Tim Berners-Lee 1990

Tim Berners-Lee also created URL, HTTP, and HTML in 1990. URL is the address of a webpage. HTTP transfers information between servers and browsers. HTML is the language used to build webpages. These tools made websites possible.

SOURCE: Wikipedia

first website Tim Berners-Lee 1991

The first website was created by Tim Berners-Lee in 1991. It explained how the World Wide Web worked. The website was hosted at CERN. It is still available online today.

SOURCE: Wikipedia

The first search engine Alan Emtage Archie index 1990

Archie was the first search engine on the Internet. It was created by Alan Emtage in 1990. Archie helped users find files stored on servers. It was an early step toward modern search engines like Google.

SOURCE: Wikipedia

The first photo on the Internet Tim Berners-Lee 1992

The first photo on the Web was uploaded in 1992. It showed a music group called Les Horribles Cernettes. It was uploaded by Tim Berners-Lee at CERN. This showed that the Web could share images, not just text.

SOURCE: Wikipedia

The invention of Wi-Fi IEEE 802.11 WIFI standard 1997

Wi-Fi allows devices to connect to the Internet without cables. The IEEE 802.11 standard was introduced in 1997. Wi-Fi made Internet access more convenient and mobile. Today, Wi-Fi is used all over the world.

SOURCE: Wikipedia
